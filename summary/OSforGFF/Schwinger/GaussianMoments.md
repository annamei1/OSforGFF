## Overview

Proves that the free Gaussian Free Field measure has finite moments of all orders,
establishing integrability of $n$-point correlation functions for complex test functions.
The central observation is that each pairing $\langle\omega, f\rangle$ is linear in $\omega$
and square-integrable (by the $L^2$ bound from `gaussianFreeField_pairing_memLp`), so
products of two pairings are $L^1$ by Hölder's inequality $L^2 \times L^2 \hookrightarrow L^1$.
The main application is `covariance_bilinear_from_general`, which proves that the
complex two-point Schwinger function of the free GFF is $\mathbb{C}$-bilinear.

### [`gaussian_pairing_product_integrable_free_2point`](../../OSforGFF/Schwinger/GaussianMoments.lean#L115) — Theorem

**Statement**: For any two complex test functions $\phi, \psi$, the product $\omega \mapsto \langle\omega,\phi\rangle_\mathbb{C}\cdot\langle\omega,\psi\rangle_\mathbb{C}$ is integrable under the free GFF measure.
