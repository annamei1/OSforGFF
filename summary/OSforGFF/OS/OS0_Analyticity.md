## Overview

Proves OS-0 (Analyticity) for the Gaussian Free Field: the generating functional
$Z[\sum_i z_i J_i] = \int \exp(i \langle \omega, \sum_i z_i J_i \rangle)\, d\mu(\omega)$
is analytic in the complex parameters $z_1, \ldots, z_n$. The strategy is to verify the
hypotheses of a multivariate holomorphic integral theorem — measurability, fiber
analyticity, integrability, and a dominated Fréchet derivative bound — and then invoke
Hartogs' theorem (axiomatized as `differentiable_analyticAt_finDim`) to pass from
everywhere-differentiability to analyticity. The derivative bound relies on Fernique's
theorem (exponential-square integrability of Gaussian pairings) together with Young's
inequality and Hölder's inequality.
